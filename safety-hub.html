Safety Hub

Essential information for a safe and worry-free journey in Georgia. From emergency numbers to road conditions.

Emergency Contacts

In Georgia, 112 is the unified emergency number for Police, Fire, and Ambulance. Operators often speak English.

  • Police: 112
  • Ambulance: 112
  • Fire: 112

Road Safety

Driving in Georgia can be challenging due to mountain terrain and aggressive local driving habits.

  • Avoid self-driving in high mountain areas (Tusheti, Svaneti) without 4x4 experience.
  • Cows often wander onto rural roads; stay alert.
  • Winter tires are mandatory from December to March in mountain zones.

Health & Water

Tap water is generally safe in Tbilisi, but bottled water is recommended for tourists to avoid stomach upset. Pharmacies (Aversi, PSP) are open 24/7 in major cities.

Tourist Tips

Georgia is very safe, but exercise caution with:

  • Taxi pricing: Always use apps (Bolt) or agree on a price beforehand.
  • Bar scams: Check menu prices before ordering in tourist-heavy nightlife areas.
