Is Georgia Safe
for Travelers?

Practical advice on crime, roads, and weather for families and solo travelers exploring the Caucasus. Travel with confidence.

Safety Guide

Georgia is consistently ranked as one of the safest countries in Europe for tourists. Violent crime is extremely rare, and hospitality is ingrained in the culture. However, being informed is key to a smooth journey.

General Safety

Crime against tourists is very low. Tbilisi and Batumi are safe to walk around day and night. Like in any major city, keep an eye on your belongings in crowded tourist spots to avoid petty theft, but generally, you can relax.

Transport & Roads

This is the main concern. Driving styles can be aggressive and erratic compared to Western Europe. If you aren't comfortable driving on mountain passes or busy city streets, we strongly recommend hiring a professional local driver.

Mountain Weather

In regions like Kazbegi, Gudauri, or Svaneti, weather changes rapidly. Roads can become blocked by snow or landslides, even in late spring. Always check conditions before traveling and ensure your vehicle is suitable for the terrain.

Family Travel

Georgians love children, and it's a very family-friendly destination. However, roadside infrastructure (like diaper changing facilities) isn't always available. Plan stops at major petrol stations or larger restaurants.
