The Heartbeat of Georgia

Tbilisi is a city of breathtaking contrasts. Here, ancient domed sulphur baths sit peacefully beneath futuristic glass pedestrian bridges. From the winding, balcony-lined streets of the Old Town to the bustling, grand Rustaveli Avenue, Tbilisi offers an unforgettable blend of history, culture, and world-famous Georgian hospitality.

The Perfect 3-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Ancient & Modern Collide

Start at Metekhi Church, take the cable car soaring up to Narikala Fortress, walk down to the famous Sulphur Baths, and end with a sunset walk across the illuminated glass Peace Bridge.

Day 2

City Culture & Cuisine

Stroll down the grand Rustaveli Avenue, visit the National Museum, explore the fascinating Dry Bridge Market for Soviet antiques, and end the night enjoying a traditional feast (Supra).

Day 3

A Mountain Escape

Take a funicular ride up to Mtatsminda Park for panoramic views of the entire city. In the afternoon, take a short drive to the colossal Chronicle of Georgia monument.
