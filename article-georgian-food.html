Top 5 Must-Try
Georgian Dishes.

A culinary journey through the flavors that define Georgia's unique kitchen. Experience the supra.

Food & Drink Guide

Visiting Georgia without trying the local cuisine is a missed opportunity. Georgian food is a feast of flavors, textures, and traditions. Here are the top dishes every traveler must experience.

01

Khinkali

Traditional dumplings served with meat or mushroom fillings. Best eaten hot. The trick is to hold them by the top knob (which isn't usually eaten), bite a small hole, suck the juices, and then enjoy the rest! It's messy, fun, and incredibly delicious.

02

Khachapuri

Georgia’s iconic cheese bread. The Adjarian style (boat-shaped with an egg) is a favorite for photos and taste, while the Imeretian style (round and closed) is the classic choice found on every table.

03

Mtsvadi

Charcoal-grilled skewers, often made of pork or veal. They are typically served with fresh onions and pomegranate seeds. Simple, smoky, and absolutely delicious, especially when paired with Georgian wine.

04

Lobio

A rich, seasoned bean stew cooked and served in a clay pot. It’s a hearty and flavorful vegetarian option, often accompanied by cornbread (Mchadi) and local pickles.

05

Pkhali

Unique vegetable appetizers made with a walnut paste base. Creating colorful balls from spinach, eggplant, or beets, they are usually served as a cold starter platter and are perfect for sharing.
