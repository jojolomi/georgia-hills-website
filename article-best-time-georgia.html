Seasonal Guide

Georgia is a true four-season destination, but depending on what you want to experience—escaping the GCC summer heat, skiing in Gudauri, or enjoying the autumn wine harvest—timing is everything.

Summer (June – August): Escaping the Heat

This is peak season for visitors from the Middle East. While Tbilisi can be quite warm (up to 35°C), the mountain regions like Kazbegi, Svaneti, and Borjomi offer incredibly refreshing, cool weather (15°C to 25°C). It is the perfect time for family road trips, hiking, and escaping the extreme desert heat.

Autumn (September – November): The Vintage Season

Autumn is widely considered the best time to visit Georgia overall. The weather is crisp and mild, the forests turn stunning shades of orange and red, and the Rtveli (harvest season) takes place in Kakheti (Signagi).

Winter (December – February): Winter Wonderland

If you want to see snow, head to Gudauri or Bakuriani. These ski resorts offer excellent facilities for families, from snowboarding to simple cable car rides over snow-capped peaks. Tbilisi is cold but beautifully decorated for the New Year celebrations, which continue until mid-January.
