Halal Food in
Georgia

A guide to Muslim-friendly dining, Halal restaurants, and vegetarian Georgian classics.

Dining Guide

Finding Halal food in Georgia is much easier than you might think. With a rich intersection of Middle Eastern and Caucasian cultures, Tbilisi and Batumi offer excellent Halal dining options.

Where to Find Halal Food in Tbilisi

The best area to find certified Halal restaurants is Marjanishvili Street (Aghmashenebeli Avenue). This bustling pedestrian street is lined with Turkish, Lebanese, and Indian restaurants serving 100% Halal meat. Many traditional Georgian restaurants in this area also source Halal meat, though it is always best to ask.

Top Halal-Friendly Georgian Dishes

  • Khachapuri: The famous Georgian cheese bread. 100% vegetarian.
  • Lobio: A rich, slow-cooked bean stew baked in a clay pot.
  • Pkhali: Vegetable and walnut pastes (spinach, beetroot, eggplant).
  • Badrijani Nigvzit: Fried eggplant rolls stuffed with walnut paste.

Batumi and the Mountains

Batumi has a strong Turkish influence due to its proximity to the border, meaning Halal Turkish restaurants are everywhere. In remote mountain regions like Kazbegi, finding certified Halal meat is harder, but vegetarian Georgian dishes and fresh local fish (like Trout) are readily available and delicious.
